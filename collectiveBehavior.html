Experience gained:

Simulating predator-prey relationships and modeling disease in this environment was a challenging but enlightening experience for me. One of the key takeaways from this project was a deeper understanding of how different ecological factors can interact and affect the dynamics of a population. Additionally, I gained knowledge about epidemiology and how disease can spread and impact different population groups.

One of the biggest challenges I faced was balancing the different ecological factors in the simulation. For example, ensuring that the predator population did not outcompete the prey population while also modeling the spread of disease required careful consideration of various parameters such as disease transmission rate, recovery rate, and population growth rate.

I also learned about the importance of considering the spatial distribution of individuals within the simulation. This was important for understanding the spread of disease and how it can impact different regions of the ecosystem.

Overall, this project was a great opportunity to learn about the interplay between different ecological factors and how disease can impact populations. I am excited to apply this knowledge to future simulations and research.
