De Stijl

1917 - 1931

History

Cubism became popular in the early 20th century, however, because the Netherlands remained neutral in World War I, Dutch artists were isolated from the rest of the art world due to the fact that they were not able to leave the country. Theo van Doesburg started to look for artists to create an art movement with, and he eventually met Piet Mondrain, who had many connections in the art world. These connections led to what we know as De Stijl, and at its height had around 100 members.

Characteristics

The main characteristics of De Stijl art are as follows:

  • Straight lines
  • Primary colors
  • Thick black lines
  • Geometric shapes

    • Colors

    As mentioned earlier, a staple of de Stijl artwork is primary colors. As such the colors red, yellow, blue, black, white, and gray are usually seen.
