Goal (taken from our report):

The goal of our analysis of The Lord of the Rings was to determine the connections characters in The Lord of the Rings have to other items or characters by creating an ontology about the film. In order to support this goal, we created a comprehensive catalog of The Lord of the Rings characters, along with the items and realms to which each character belongs. Looking through the resources currently available, most are considerably narrow and do not cover the large number of characters which are in the canon created by J. R. R. Tolkien. Resources such as Wikipedia or Lotro-Wiki.com offer a decent amount of information on the different characters and the universe in which they exist, but fail to represent the relations between these characters and objects (bonds that they might have, characteristics that they might share, etc.). As such, our ontology aimed to alleviate this deficiency within the realm of this series.

Experience gained:

I ended up doing a lot of the work for this project, including all of the data visualization, most of the mapping of data and conceptualization, all of the SPARQL queries, and most of the actual writing in our final report. I had very busy teammates, which was unfortunate; however I was able to take initiative, and because I did so much of the project by myself, I was able to learn a lot about every aspect this project covered.
